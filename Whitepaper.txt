SFOX Network Tokenomics
Abstract
We propose a gamified tokenomics model for SFOX that combines a time-based halving emission
schedule with a multi-tier referral bonus system. Mining begins at 1 SFOX per hour per user, with the base
rate halving every two weeks to enforce rapid scarcity and reward early adopters. In parallel, users gain
permanent rank multipliers and per-referral mining boosts as they recruit active participants. Each rank
(Pioneer, Lord, Baron, President, Titan) unlocks a large base reward boost (150–5000%) and a small
percentage bonus per active referral above a fixed “free capacity” threshold. Only active (daily mining)
referrals count, ensuring real engagement. This hybrid design (inspired by models like Pi and Tau Network
) creates strong viral incentives and fast expansion while capping total supply via aggressive
halvings.
Introduction
Many recent networks (e.g. Pi, Tau) use referral and trust mechanisms to drive growth . Our goal is
to leverage these concepts to bootstrap SFOX adoption: early miners receive outsized rewards, but the
system rapidly decays per-user issuance to prevent runaway inflation. The halving schedule introduces
strict scarcity (modeled after Bitcoin and social-mining projects ), while the rank/referral tiers gamify
participation and reward community builders. Altogether, this tokenomics enforces virality with control: only
genuine expansion beyond set thresholds generates extra tokens, limiting concentration and inflation risk.
Emission Schedule (Halving)
Base Rate: 1 SFOX per hour, per active miner at launch.
Halving: Every 2 weeks, the base rate is cut in half (e.g. after 14 days from launch, 1 SFOX/h → 0.5
SFOX/h, then 0.25, etc.). This fixed-period halving mimics mechanisms in Pi and other projects
 to produce geometric decay in supply. (Equivalently, after n halvings the rate is $1/2^n$ SFOX/h.)
Effect: This schedule yields extreme scarcity: only a few hundred thousand SFOX are minted in the
first few months even with network growth. (For example, a simple model with linear user growth
yields ≈0.14 million SFOX after 180 days.) The aggressive decay means the network must rapidly
expand to sustain any mining output, aligning incentives with early adoption .
Token Supply: By analogy to other token designs, we can define a maximum supply S split into
(M=Mining, R=Referral, D=Development). For example, Pi used a formula S = M+R+D with R
proportional to M . We propose similarly allocating a fixed percentage to team/developers
(e.g. 20%), with 80% for community mining. All mined SFOX come only from the mining pool; there
are no private sales or unlocks.
This halving-driven issuance has precedent: Ziktalk’s whitepaper defines dual halvings by time and by user
growth , and Pi Network halves rewards at adoption milestones . In both cases, new supply slows
dramatically as the network scales, which balances the high referral-based inflation in early phases.

Rank-Based Referral System
Every SFOX user is automatically in a referral tree. When a user (“referrer”) signs up new participants
(“referees”) who actively mine, the referrer permanently increases their mining rate. The system has 5 ranks
with thresholds and bonuses:
Pioneer (Early Adopter) – Requirement: join in first 30 days. Rank Boost: +150% (i.e. ×2.5 mining).
Referral Bonus: +10% per active referral above 10. (First 10 referrals yield no extra boost.)
Lord – Requirement: ≥10 active referrals. Rank Boost: +500% (×6.0). Referral Bonus: +12% per active
referral above 50. (First 50 referrals are “structural” and give no extra.)
Baron – Requirement: ≥50 active referrals. Rank Boost: +1500% (×16.0). Referral Bonus: +15% per
active referral above 100. (First 100 referrals give no extra.)
President – Requirement: ≥100 active referrals. Rank Boost: +3000% (×31.0). Referral Bonus: +20% per
active referral above 500. (First 500 referrals no extra.)
Titan – Requirement: ≥500 active referrals. Rank Boost: +5000% (×51.0). Referral Bonus: +25% per
active referral above 500. (Only referrals #501+ generate bonus.)
Each rank’s “free capacity” threshold means that only referrals beyond that number yield incremental
bonus. For instance, a Titan with 800 active referrals has 300 “overflow” referrals: bonus = 300×25% =
+7500% (i.e. ×76.0 mining). If only 700 are active, overflow = 200 → +5000% (×51.0); 500 active referrals
yields overflow=0 (×51.0 mining). This overflow rule prevents small teams from compounding unlimited
referral benefits. All referrals must be active (mining daily) – inactive referrals count for 0% bonus.
Mining Rate Formula
Let B = base rate (SFOX/hour, with halving), P = rank multiplier (e.g. +50.0 for Titan’s +5000%), r = per-referral
bonus (0.10–0.25), C = free-capacity threshold, and A = max(active_referrals – C, 0). Then each user’s mining
rate is:
R = B × (1 + P + r × A)
All additions above are linear fractions of B. For example, a Lord (P=5.0) with 60 active referrals (C=50, A=10)
mines at
$R = B × (1 + 5.0 + 0.12×10) = B × (1 + 5.0 + 1.2) = 7.2 B$.
If $B=1$ SFOX/h, that is 7.2 SFOX/h. In contrast, a Pioneer (P=1.5) with 10 active referrals (all within free
capacity) gets $R = B×(1+1.5) = 2.5 B$ (no referral bonus). See Table below for rank details.

Incentive Effects
This tiered system yields strong viral growth incentives and social competition. Early users (Pioneers) get a
permanent boost for being first movers. Those who recruit many users climb ranks and achieve
exponential scaling: beyond each threshold, every additional active referral adds a multiplicative boost.
Critically, the overflow-only rule ensures that small groups cannot exploit referral loops: only growth above
each rank’s base network yields rewards. As a result, the “Titan” rank remains an elite goal — only teams
that recruit 501+ beyond 500 (i.e. 1,001+ total) reach it, reinforcing its prestige and making expansion
necessary to maintain power.
Only active referrals count toward bonuses. Inactivity resets one’s effective bonus (contribution=0) for that
day. This aligns mining rewards with real engagement and thwarts passive or “zombie” accounts.
This design aligns with principles seen in other projects: for example, Tau Network explicitly ties referral
rewards to mining supply (R = r·M) , and Pi’s whitepaper similarly allocates referral tokens to share
growth incentives . By capping referral rewards to overflow growth, we avoid unchecked compounding
that would otherwise create infinite issuance loops.
Economic Analysis
Under this model, early phases produce relatively high output per miner, but output per user rapidly
declines due to halvings. For instance, after just 4 halvings (8 weeks), the base rate is only 1/16 its original
value. Thus, even a Titan’s gross rate collapses over time unless new users join to replace falling base. In
practice, aggressive halving caps total SFOX supply. (A rough simulation shows under linear growth the
majority of supply is minted in the first 2–3 months; after ~6 months nearly all emission ceases.)
Referrals can amplify short-term rewards: a Titan with overflow bonuses mines ~51× base (plus referral
extra). However, because base decays exponentially, all rewards plateau. For example, a Titan with 500
active referrals initially mines 176 SFOX/h; after 14 days this drops to 88 SFOX/h, then 44, then 22, etc. Even
continuous recruitment only slightly raises this tail. Hence long-term inflation remains bounded.
Nonetheless, the multipliers are extreme, so unchecked they could cause early centralization. As ranks
shift slowly (only based on active referrals), top builders accumulate far more SFOX than latecomers.

Moreover, if the network stalls, later signups get almost no reward. To mitigate these risks, we recommend
the following anti-inflation and stability measures:
Fixed Supply Cap: Define a maximum token supply (e.g. S SFOX). Mining halts when reached. Similar
to Pi’s eventual 100 billion cap , a cap prevents infinite issuance.
Diminishing Returns: Beyond Tier 5, reduce incremental bonuses. For example, after 1,000 total
referrals, each extra active referral yields a smaller boost than +25%. This limits runaway growth
from large teams.
Referral Bonus Decay: Gradually lower r for each rank after some time or total team size, to shrink
the gap between early/late joiners.
Burn Mechanism: Sink a portion of harvested SFOX (e.g. a small burn on transfers or an “unlock
schedule”) to counter inflation.
Retention Locks: Introduce time-locks (vesting) on rewards so that rapid mining surges do not
immediately flood the market.
These controls are common in modern token designs. Our whitepaper model already enforces some
discipline via halvings, but additional guards will ensure economic stability and fairness as the community
grows.
Conclusion
The SFOX tokenomics model merges a deflationary halving emission schedule with an overflow-based
referral engine. This design rewards early community builders with outsized mining power while tightly
bounding total supply. It creates competitive leaderboards and urgency around each halving event,
leveraging network effects for rapid growth. At the same time, the free-capacity rule and active-only
condition prevent simple compounding and sybil-style inflation. If paired with a clear supply cap and bonus
tapering, this model can achieve viral adoption without collapsing the economy.
Next steps could include formalizing the supply curve, drafting detailed token distribution (e.g. 80% to
miners, 20% to team as in [17]), and building a dashboard to track ranks and emissions. Simulation of
various growth scenarios will further refine anti-inflation parameters and ensure the system’s long-term
sustainability.

referral rewards can coexist to bootstrap a decentralized economy while capping inflation.
